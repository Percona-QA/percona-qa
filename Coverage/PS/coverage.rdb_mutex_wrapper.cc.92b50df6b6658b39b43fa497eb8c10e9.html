132 The manual page says ( http://linux.die.net/man/3/pthread_cond_signal):
133
134 The pthread_cond_broadcast() or pthread_cond_signal() functions may be called
135 by a thread whether or not it currently owns the mutex that threads calling
136 pthread_cond_wait() or pthread_cond_timedwait() have associated with the
137 condition variable during their waits; however, IF PREDICTABLE SCHEDULING
138 BEHAVIOR IS REQUIRED, THEN THAT MUTEX SHALL BE LOCKED by the thread calling
139 pthread_cond_broadcast() or pthread_cond_signal().
140
141 What's "predicate scheduling" and do we need it? The explanation is here:
142
143 https://groups.google.com/forum/?hl=ky#!msg/comp.programming.threads/wEUgPq541v8/ZByyyS8acqMJ
144 "The problem (from the realtime side) with condition variables is that

147 without reference to the condition variable, then you can have an
148 indirect priority inversion."
